# LinkedIn Algorithm Guidelines (360Brew Era)

> Distilled from the [360Brew Deep-Dive Report](../LinkedIn%20360Brew%20Deep-Dive%20Report.md). Last updated: March 2026.
>
> **Purpose**: Post-writing rules only. For profile optimization, networking strategy, and daily behaviours, see the full report.

## How 360Brew Evaluates Posts

The algorithm semantically reads your post text, your profile, and the viewer's profile, then decides: _"Is this professionally relevant and valuable for this specific person?"_

It does **not** optimize for raw engagement volume. It optimizes for depth, relevance, and professional value.

## What Gets Rewarded

### Content Signals

- **Clarity of topic** — Posts that clearly communicate what they're about and who they're for rank higher. Naming the audience up-front helps (e.g., "For ops leaders dealing with...").
- **Depth and structure** — Problem → explanation → steps → takeaway. Structured posts improve dwell time and comprehension. Thin or vague posts struggle.
- **Problem-solving content** — Posts that solve specific problems, share frameworks/checklists, or break down processes drive saves and meaningful comments.
- **Authentic, experience-based writing** — Real projects, failures, and decisions stand out vs. generic AI-generated platitudes.
- **Niche consistency** — Posts aligned with your profile's declared expertise get routed to the right audience. Stay in your 2–3 topic lanes.

### Engagement Signals (ranked by weight)

1. **Dwell time** — Time spent reading after "See more," scroll depth, carousel swipes. Write for reading depth.
2. **Comments** — Thoughtful, multi-sentence comments and reply threads. Author replies are particularly valuable.
3. **Saves** — Indicates long-term value. Posts with high save rates get slow-burn extended reach.
4. **Reposts with commentary** — Stronger than blind reshares.
5. **Profile visits / connection requests** — Proxy for real professional value.
6. **Likes / reactions** — Still counted but downgraded relative to everything above.

### Post Longevity

- Posts can resurface over several days (not just 24h) if comments remain active, saves are strong, and the topic stays timely.
- Responding to early comments quickly and with 2–3 sentence replies amplifies the expansion signal.

## What Gets Suppressed

- **Em dashes (—)** — Avoid entirely. Widely recognized as an AI writing pattern. Use a period, comma, or restructured sentence instead.
- **Engagement bait** — "Comment 'yes' for the template," mass-tagging unrelated people, polarizing takes designed only to farm reactions.
- **Hashtags** — No longer a distribution lever. Do not use them.
- **Low-value AI spam** — Generic, templated posts without original insight see weaker distribution.
- **Off-topic content** — Posts mismatched with your profile's niche weaken topical authority and make distribution erratic.
- **Link-drops** — Short posts that are just a link + tagline underperform. Wrap links in a value-dense summary.

## Format Guidelines

| Format             | When it works                                                     | When it doesn't                                   |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| **Text (short)**   | Insights, opinions, hot takes with clear structure                | Vague or generic — needs specifics to drive dwell |
| **Text (long)**    | Stories, frameworks, case studies with clear sections             | Walls of text without whitespace or structure     |
| **Carousel**       | Frameworks, checklists, visual breakdowns — drives swipes + saves | Low-effort slide decks with no real content       |
| **Video**          | Teaches or demonstrates something, strong watch time              | Static talking-head clips with low completion     |
| **External links** | Wrapped in 3–5 key takeaways with audience context                | Posted as bare link + one-liner                   |

## CTA Design

- **Do**: Ask specific questions that invite detailed replies — _"If you've tried X, what broke first?"_
- **Don't**: Use lazy CTAs — _"Thoughts?"_, _"Agree?"_, _"Drop a 🔥 if..."_
- The algo values comment depth (multi-sentence, multi-turn threads), so design CTAs that provoke real answers.
