# SEO Guidelines — Blog Content

> **Purpose**: SEO rules for blog articles on the personal branding site. Analogous to `algorithm.md` for LinkedIn.

## Keyword Strategy

### Target Keywords

Each article has **one primary keyword** and **2–3 supporting keywords**.

- **Primary keyword**: The main search term you want to rank for. Include in:
  - Article title
  - First 100 words
  - At least 2 H2 headings
  - Conclusion
  - Meta description
  - URL slug
- **Supporting keywords**: Related terms and variations. Distribute naturally through body sections. Don't force them — if they fit, use them.

### Keyword Selection

- Choose keywords aligned with your content pillars (AI agents, operations, revenue impact, career growth)
- Prioritize long-tail keywords (3–5 words) over broad single-word terms — less competition, higher intent
- Think reader-first: "What would someone type into Google if they needed this article?"
- Examples:
  - Broad (avoid): "AI automation"
  - Long-tail (prefer): "AI agents for operations teams" or "how to automate MSP workflows with AI"

### Keyword Density

- No target percentage. If the keyword reads as forced anywhere, rewrite that sentence.
- Natural integration always beats mechanical placement.

## Heading Structure

- **H1**: Article title only. One per page.
- **H2**: Major sections (3–6 per article). Each should be a clear sub-topic.
- **H3**: Sub-points within an H2 section. Use when a section has 2+ distinct parts.
- **No skipped levels**: Don't jump from H1 to H3.
- **Include keywords in headings**: At least 2 H2s should incorporate the primary or supporting keywords naturally.

## Meta Descriptions

- **Length**: 145–155 characters
- **Formula**: [Value proposition] + [specificity] + [keyword]
- **Include**: Target keyword, a clear benefit, enough specificity to differentiate from other results
- **Avoid**: Generic descriptions, keyword stuffing, starting with "This article..."

Examples:
- Good: "Learn how operations leaders are using AI agents to automate client onboarding and cut time-to-value by 60%."
- Bad: "This article discusses AI agents and automation for business operations improvement."

## Content Structure

### Readability

- Paragraphs: 2–4 sentences max
- Sections: 150–300 words each. If a section exceeds 300 words, break it into subsections
- Sentence variety: Mix short declarative sentences with medium-length explanatory ones
- Use bullet lists and numbered steps for scanability
- Bold key phrases the reader is scanning for

### Internal Linking

- Reference other published articles when relevant
- Use descriptive anchor text (not "click here")
- Link early in the article when possible — it signals depth to search engines
- Don't force links. If there's no natural connection, skip it.

### Images and Alt Text

- Use descriptive alt text that includes the keyword where natural
- Alt text should describe what the image shows, not just repeat the keyword
- Every image should serve a purpose (diagram, screenshot, data visualization) — no decorative stock photos

## E-E-A-T Signals

Google prioritizes content that demonstrates Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness:

- **Experience**: Write from real projects, real outcomes, real timelines. Name tools and specifics.
- **Expertise**: Cover topics within your declared pillars. Don't write outside your lane.
- **Authoritativeness**: Use concrete data, frameworks, before/after contrasts. Cite sources when referencing third-party data.
- **Trustworthiness**: Be honest about limitations and trade-offs. Acknowledge what you don't know.

## What to Avoid

- **Keyword stuffing**: If a sentence reads awkwardly because of keyword placement, rewrite it.
- **Thin content**: Articles under 1,000 words rarely rank. Aim for 1,500–2,500 words of substantive content.
- **Duplicate angles**: Don't publish multiple articles targeting the same keyword with the same angle.
- **AI-generated filler**: Generic paragraphs that don't add insight. Every section earns its place.
- **Missing meta descriptions**: Every article needs one. Search engines will auto-generate a bad one if you don't.
