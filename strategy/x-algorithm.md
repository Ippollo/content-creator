# X Algorithm & Dynamics (2026)

This document captures the current operating mechanics of the X algorithm, particularly for "Build in Public" and technical content.

## The Core Metric: Session Depth & Time-on-Platform

X no longer optimizes purely for raw likes or retweets. The algorithm disproportionately rewards **time spent** and **session depth**. 

Posts that make users pause their scroll, read thoroughly, and expand the replies are prioritized.

### Key Engagement Weights
1. **Replies (High Value)**: Genuine conversation. "Reply prompts" (like "Agree or disagree?") work if they don't feel like engagement bait.
2. **Bookmarks (High Value)**: Signals high-value, reference-grade content. Easy-to-quote lines and numbered insights drive bookmarks.
3. **Reposts (Medium Value)**: Expands reach, but is heavily filtered for spam behavior.
4. **Likes (Low Value)**: Baseline engagement, but insufficient on its own to go viral.

## Post Mechanics & Constraints

### Character Limits & Optimal Lengths
- **The Sweet Spot (71-140 chars)**: Yields maximum baseline engagement. Fast, punchy, skimmable.
- **Value-Packed Singles (Up to 280 chars)**: Allowed if every word earns its place.
- **Threads**: Best used sparingly. Must have a standalone hook tweet, followed by numbered insights, personal proof, and a concluding TL;DR / CTA. Soft default is 1-3 tweets, with a hard ceiling of 10.

### The Penalty Box
- **External Links**: Links in the main post body carry a heavy penalty because they push users off-platform. If a link is necessary, deliver the core value directly in the post.
- **Corporate Speak**: "Excited to announce" is actively filtered by users (and likely the algorithm's semantic models) as low-value promotional content.
- **"Hashtag Stuffing"**: Degrades post quality. Zero or one highly targeted hashtag is the maximum penalty-free limit.

## Proven X-Native Patterns

The algorithm favors specific content structures that drive the metrics above.

### The E.H.A. Framework
The foundation of a good X post is: **Emotional Trigger → Hook → Action**.
- *Example Trigger*: Frustration with common advice.
- *Example Hook*: "Everyone says post every day. I grew 10x faster by posting 3x/week."
- *Example Action*: Read the thread, reply, or bookmark.

### High-Converting Thread Openers
A strong thread opener introduces a problem, establishes personal stakes, and creates a curiosity gap.
> *"I spent 5 years building products that flopped before one finally hit $1M ARR. The difference wasn't luck—it was one mindset shift nobody talks about. 🧵"*

### Contrarian Value Posts
These posts contrast common "best practices" with actual builder reality, optimized for bookmarks and replies.
> *"Everyone says 'post every day' to grow on X. I grew 10x faster by posting 3x/week with better hooks. Quantity is a trap. Here's what actually compounds: [quick list]. Agree or disagree?"*
