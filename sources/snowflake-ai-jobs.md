### Key Claims
- **AI-driven job creation outpaces job cuts**: 77% of organizations experienced AI-driven job creation, compared to 46% reporting reductions. Among organizations experiencing both, 69% report a positive net workforce effect. (Snowflake/Omdia study)
- **Technical roles lead job growth but also experience high disruption**: IT operations saw the highest levels of both job creation (56% gains) and job loss, while customer service/support (37%) and data analytics (37%) were highly affected by reductions. (Snowflake/Omdia study)
- **Unstructured data is the primary scaling bottleneck**: 96% of organizations face challenges scaling AI, with 65% struggling with data silos and 62% struggling to measure AI data quality. Only 7% say more than half of their unstructured data is AI-ready. (Snowflake/Omdia study)
- **Widespread shadow AI usage, led by executives**: 57% of employees admit to using unapproved AI tools, and this number rises to 66% among C-level leaders. (Snowflake/Omdia study)
- **AI ROI is positive for mature adopters**: Organizations earn an average of $1.49 for every dollar invested in AI; 92% of early adopters report positive returns. (Snowflake/Omdia study)
